# Tim's Lesson Production Guide

How to make a lesson that works, end to end.

You're the Fortnite expert here. This doc is just the platform side. The format, the length, the few rules. Read it once. Don't try to memorize it. The AI suggest buttons in the admin do half the work.

---

## What goes into a lesson

A lesson is **7 text fields** plus **2 file types**.

The 7 text fields (you type these in `/admin/lessons/new`):

1. **Title.** Internal only. Just for you to find it later. Example: `Tunneling basics` or `Box fight openers`.
2. **Fortnite label.** What the kid sees on their portal. Usually the same as the title. Use Fortnite words freely here. Example: `Tunneling`.
3. **Parent label.** What the parent sees in their weekly email. Translate the Fortnite skill into a real world skill a parent cares about. Tap the **Suggest** button to get an AI draft. Edit it so it sounds like you. Example: `Staying calm when a fight gets fast`.
4. **Parent skill description.** One sentence explaining what skill this builds. Same field as #3, AI suggests this too. Example: `Helps your kid keep their head when someone's pushing them, and make a plan instead of panicking.`
5. **Topic.** Pick one: building, editing, aim, game sense, mental, tournament prep.
6. **Difficulty.** Pick one: beginner, intermediate, advanced, unreal.
7. **Duration in minutes.** Rough total audio length. 5 to 8 usually.

The 2 file types:

- **Slide images.** PNG files exported from Google Slides. One per slide.
- **Audio files.** MP3 files recorded in QuickTime. One per slide.

You can also add **speaker notes** per slide (optional). These are short notes the kid sees if they want extra context. Keep them short.

## How long should a lesson be?

- **4 to 6 slides.**
- **30 to 90 seconds of audio per slide.**
- **5 to 8 minutes total lesson length.**

Why these numbers:

- Fewer than 3 slides feels thin for what we charge ($14 per lesson, $56 per 4 lesson cycle).
- More than 8 slides and the kid checks out.
- Audio under 20 seconds per slide feels rushed.
- Audio over 2 minutes per slide feels like a lecture and the kid skips it.

**Don't pad a lesson to hit a number.** A lesson that teaches one thing clearly in 4 slides beats one that teaches the same thing badly in 8.

## Parent talking points (the secret sauce)

Every lesson needs **5 short lines**, one per category. These go into the parent's weekly email. They give the parent something specific to say to the kid that shows the parent actually knows what's going on in the game.

This is the part that makes XPL Keyed different from every other coaching service. Take it seriously.

The 5 categories:

- **Informed observer.** Something the parent can notice during the kid's session.
  - Example: *"Tim said you're working on tunneling this week. That's when you build cover while still tracking the other guy. Watch for it next time you play."*

- **Co conspirator.** You and the parent are on the same team. The parent quotes you.
  - Example: *"Tim told me to ask if you've stopped W keying yet. He says it's still a problem."*

- **Cultural literacy.** The parent shows they know what's actually happening in the game.
  - Example: *"How did your endgame go today? Were you sweating or cracked?"*

- **Good question.** Open ended question that gets the kid talking.
  - Example: *"What's the play you're most proud of from this week?"*

- **Strategic note.** Something specific the parent can comment on that signals real understanding.
  - Example: *"Nice retake on that last fight. You held the high ground instead of pushing."*

**The AI suggest button can draft all 5 in one click.** Use it, then edit. Make them sound like you. Three tone rules:

1. The parent **asks**. They don't perform. Don't write scripts that make the parent quote slang they can't pronounce.
2. You and the parent are partners. Never make the parent the butt of a joke.
3. The audience is the parent. The kid is the third party the parent is trying to connect with.

## The dash rule

Anything a parent or kid reads must NOT contain any of these characters:

- Em dash `—`
- En dash `–`
- Hyphen `-`

That includes parent labels, skill descriptions, talking points, and any speaker notes a parent might see.

Use periods, commas, "to," or spaces instead.

Examples:

- `Unreal-ranked` becomes `Unreal ranked`
- `30-min lesson` becomes `30 min lesson`
- `Mon–Tue` becomes `Mon to Tue`
- `Defensive building under pressure — trains spatial planning` becomes `Defensive building under pressure. Trains spatial planning.`

You CAN still use hyphens in internal fields (the title, your slide file names, your local notes). Just not in anything labeled "parent" or that ends up in an email.

## Workflow

1. Decide on the lesson. Pick ONE specific skill, not a category. `Tunneling basics` is good. `Building` is too broad.
2. Build the slides in Google Slides. 4 to 6 slides. Each slide focuses on one beat.
3. Record audio for each slide separately in QuickTime. Save each as MP3 (use a free converter if QuickTime saves as `.m4a`).
4. Export each slide as a PNG: **File → Download → PNG image**.
5. Go to xplkeyed.com, sign in as coach, click **Lessons → Author a new lesson**.
6. Fill in the 7 text fields. Tap the **Suggest** buttons for the parent label, skill description, and talking points. Edit the output until it sounds like you.
7. Add slides one at a time. Each slide gets a PNG file, an MP3 file, and optional speaker notes.
8. Toggle **Published** on, click **Save**.
9. Lesson is now in the library. It can be assigned to any kid's curriculum.

## What good looks like

A great XPL Keyed lesson:

- Teaches one specific thing the kid can practice that same day.
- Shows examples from actual gameplay, not just theory.
- Sounds like you in the audio. Conversational. Not a script being read.
- Has parent talking points that feel honest and warm, not corporate.

Things to avoid:

- Trying to cover too many concepts in one lesson. Split them.
- Slides with walls of text. Use images and short labels.
- Audio that sounds rushed because you tried to fit too much in.
- Parent talking points that read like marketing copy.

---

## Production cadence

Aim for **1 to 2 lessons per week** until you have 5 to 10 in the library. Then we can start onboarding the first family.

Don't burn out producing all 10 at once. Quality over quantity. The first 5 lessons set the bar for everything that follows.
