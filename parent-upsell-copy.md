## Length 2: Full "Why this works" section (above the intake form)

### Why coached Fortnite actually builds your kid

**1. Action gaming sharpens measurable cognitive skills.**
Decades of research from the Bavelier lab at the University of Rochester `[VERIFY]` has shown that action video game players outperform non gamers on tests of visual attention, task switching, and perceptual decision making, without losing accuracy. The foundational paper (Green & Bavelier, *Nature*, 2003) `[VERIFY]` has been followed by many replications and extensions. **Confidence: HIGH.**

**2. Action gaming improves 3D spatial reasoning.**
A University of Toronto study (Feng, Spence & Pratt, *Psychological Science*, 2007) `[VERIFY]` found that action game training improved mental rotation performance in non gamers and reduced the longstanding gender gap in spatial cognition, a skill linked to STEM achievement. **Confidence: HIGH.**

**3. Coaching is what turns gaming time into growth time.**
The biggest predictor of skill growth in any domain is targeted feedback from someone more skilled, the principle of deliberate practice (Ericsson and colleagues) `[VERIFY]`. Hours spent playing aimlessly compound slowly; hours spent playing with a coach who watches your gameplay and tells you exactly what to fix compound fast. Same loop that makes piano lessons better than YouTube tutorials. **Confidence: HIGH** for deliberate practice generally; **MEDIUM** for the direct game coaching transfer (less studied formally, but the underlying principle is robust).

**4. Tournament grade play teaches what school doesn't.**
Performing under time pressure. Recovering from losses without spiraling. Setting measurable goals. Taking feedback without ego. These are exactly what competitive Fortnite at the Unreal level demands, and they transfer to athletics, test taking, and any high stakes performance setting. **Confidence: MEDIUM.** Well supported in sports psychology research broadly; less formal study specifically on esports athletes, though the underlying mechanics are the same. `[VERIFY]`

**5. Esports is a legitimate pathway.**
US colleges with varsity esports programs award millions in annual scholarships through NACE (National Association of Collegiate Esports). `[VERIFY: current count of varsity programs and current annual scholarship total. These figures change year over year. NEEDS CURRENT DATA from the latest NACE annual report or equivalent.]` Pro play, content creation, and coaching are real (if small) career paths, earned the same way any athletic scholarship is: through coached deliberate practice. **Confidence: HIGH** that the pathway exists; **NEEDS CURRENT DATA** on every specific number.

### What we don't claim

- **Gaming alone won't raise your kid's grades.** No serious research supports that direct claim. We're selling coached, structured gaming with a real curriculum, not gaming as tutor. `[VERIFY: confirm there's no recent meta analysis showing transfer to academic performance. Last I checked, transfer effects are domain specific.]`
- **"Hand eye coordination" is real but oversold.** It's mostly a vague stand in for the more specific perceptual and attentional skills above.
- **Effect sizes are real but moderate.** Some published critiques (Boot, Blakely & Simons and others, 2008 to 2013) `[VERIFY]` have argued that some action video game effects are smaller than the headlines suggest, or driven by methodological issues (recruitment, expectancy). The honest summary: real benefits, real replication, but not a magic bullet.
